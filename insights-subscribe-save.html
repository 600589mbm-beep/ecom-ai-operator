The Chewy playbook: turn buyers into subscribers

Chewy converts around 6% — double the all-store average — by making the natural choice a recurring one. Any replenishable product can run the same engine.

A one-time sale is a transaction. A subscription is an asset. Chewy's Autoship works because pet owners will rebuy the same food and litter — so removing the re-order decision is a gift, not a trick. Here's how to build that for your store.

1 · Find what's actually replenishable

Coffee, supplements, skincare, refills, consumables, pet supplies — anything bought on a cycle. If a customer needs it again in 30–60 days, it's a subscription candidate. One-and-done products are not; don't force it.

2 · Make the subscribe option the easy, cheaper one

Default the product page to "Subscribe & save 10–15%," with one-time purchase as the secondary choice. The discount is cheaper than re-acquiring the customer with ads every cycle.

3 · Nail the cadence

Let the customer pick the interval, and pre-fill a sensible default (a 30-day product → 30-day refill). Wrong cadence is the top cancel reason — too soon feels wasteful, too late means they buy elsewhere.

4 · Build the lifecycle emails

Subscriptions live or die on communication: a "shipping soon" heads-up (reduces surprise-charge disputes), an easy skip/swap link, and a win-back when someone cancels. The Email Flow Writer produces all three.

5 · Reduce churn with proactive support

Make pausing easier than cancelling. Answer "can I change my next shipment?" instantly. The Support Bot turns those into one-tap resolutions instead of cancellations.

6 · Sell the subscription in the listing itself

State the savings and the "never run out" benefit right in the copy. The Listing Optimizer can write a subscribe-and-save variant of any listing.

The math: a subscriber who reorders four times is worth 4× a one-time buyer at roughly the same acquisition cost. That's why Chewy can spend confidently to acquire — lifetime value, not first-order profit, funds the growth.
